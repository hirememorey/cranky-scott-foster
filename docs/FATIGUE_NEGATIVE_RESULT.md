# Fatigue And Workload Negative Result

This project began as a fatigue, travel, and pace investigation. The current evidence does not support broad schedule-load variables as the main explanation for NBA late-game officiating errors in L2M-reviewed events.

## Defensible Finding

Broad workload proxies such as back-to-backs, recent travel miles, time-zone crossings, and pace/action density do not explain late-game error risk nearly as well as structural decision context.

## How To Use This In The Paper

Use fatigue as a rejected initial hypothesis and a credibility check. The analysis should say that the data pointed away from the original travel/fatigue framing and toward a more specific event-context model.

## What Not To Claim

Do not claim that fatigue never affects officiating. The available evidence only says these broad proxies do not materially improve prediction of L2M-reviewed incorrect decisions in the current setup.

Do not use this result to rank individual referees. The strongest signal is decision context, not referee identity.
